Kuku Sabzi

Description

Kuku is a dish made of whipped eggs folded in various ingredients. It is served traditionally at Nowruz, symbolizing a fresh start.

Ingredients

  • 5 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 1 medium onion
  • 1 medium leek,finely chopped
  • 5 large eggs
  • 1 + 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 + 1/2 cups finely chopped cilantro
  • 1 + 1/2 cups finely chopped dill
  • 1 + 1/2 cups finely chopped parsley
  • 1 tablespoon dried fenugreek leaves

Steps

  1. Heat 2 Tbsp of oil in a skillet over medium. Cook onion and leek, stirring occasionally, until very soft but not brown, 10-12 minutes. Transfer to a plate and let cool. Wipe out skillet; set aside.
  2. Whisk eggs, salt, baking powder, pepper, and turmeric in a large bowl. Using a rubber spatula, mix in onion mixture, cilantro, dill, parsley, and fenugreek.
  3. Heat Broiler. Heat remaining 3 Tbsp oil in reserved skillet over medium. Pour in egg mixture; spread evenly across pan with spatula. Cover and cook frittata until bottom is just set, 8-10 minutes. Uncover and broil, watching carefully, until top is set, about 1 minute. Let cool slightly, then slide out onto a platter.
